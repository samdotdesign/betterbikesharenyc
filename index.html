Citi Bike must prioritize service in underprivileged neighborhoods.

Neighborhoods on the outskirts of Citi Bike’s service area experience lower reliability compared to those closer to the center. These areas are often home to Black, Latinx, and low-income residents. The result is an inequitable public service.

Together, we can push for change to ensure every resident has a reliable and affordable biking option.

The problems

Unreliable service on the edges of the network

  • Neighborhoods on the edge of the network suffer more from unusable stations. This disproportionately affects Black, Latinx, and low-income residents.
  • Rebalancing bikes dropped 80% from 2014-2019 to 2022.
  • Riders in the Bronx are 89% more likely to encounter unusable stations compared to other boroughs.

Lack of contract enforcement

  • Despite numerous service failures, the city hasn’t enforced the performance standards agreed by Lyft.

High cost

  • Current discounts for NYCHA and SNAP recipients are great, but they are inadequate and convoluted for many low-income New Yorkers.

The solutions

  • Lyft, who operates Citi Bike, must prioritize maintenance in underserved areas to ensure equitable service.
  • The city needs neighborhood-level performance standards for Lyft to follow. The results should be shared publicly.
  • The city must respond to Lyft’s performance with fines when compliance isn’t met, and rewards when performance is good.
  • Expand and simplify eligibility for discounted memberships to reach more low-income residents.

This is a neighbor-run campaign of transportation and climate activists. Equitable bike share is key for the future of low-carbon transit in NYC.
